Alfredo Sauce

Description

Alfredo sauce is one of my favorite meals. The ingredients are items that stay good for a long time, so you can keep them around for when you need them. Some nights if I do not have a plan for dinner and I need something quick, alfredo saves the day.

This recipe whips up in no time at all. When you are ready to add the pasta to the water, it's time to start the sauce. Everything comes together nicely in the end and it's time to eat!

Ingredients

  • One pound of pasta of choice
  • One stick of butter
  • One bar of cream cheese (8 oz.)
  • One cup of milk
  • One cup of Parmesan cheese
  • Garlic powder if you like it Garlicky

Steps:

  1. Fill a large pot with water, leaving a few inches of space at the top. Bring water to a boil and add the pasta. Cook for 8-12 minutes.
  2. In a medium sauce pan over medium heat, melt together the stick of butter and bar of cream cheese. You will need to stir together well and wait for all of the cream cheese to melt.
  3. Add milk and Parmesan cheese to the pan and stir to combine. Continue to cook for a few minutes until the sauce is smooth. Add Garlic powder now if you want it.
  4. When pasta is tender and to your liking, remove from heat and strain.
  5. Top pasta with Alfredo sauce.
  6. Serve and enjoy!
